July Feature Story

Summer is officially here! In the Scenic Wild Delaware River (SWDR) region, summertime is synonymous with peak travel season. It is time to hit the road and explore the great things the Scenic Wild Delaware River program has to offer. The SWDR region has something that people of all ages and interests can enjoy.

There are many opportunities to try your hand at something new in the SWDR region. Our partners offer unique experiences from as fly-fishing lessons to zip lining tours. Many towns along the SWDR have historic museums, theaters, and points of interests to tour. With an abundance of places to stay, from campgrounds on the banks of the river to beautiful B&Bs with many amenities, start planning your trip now!
Please visit our website https://delawareriver.natgeotourism.com to read more on where to stay, places to go, and fun things to do. Continue to check our website for special events, such as music festivals, farmers markets, and exhibits occurring throughout the summer.

Around the SWDR Region - In The News

The National Parks Conservation Association and the Scenic Wild Delaware River program were co-sponsors of the recent Upper Delaware River Bio Blitz event. For the second time in five years, the Bio Blitz was held on the Gales Property in Starlight, Wayne County, PA. At this two-day event, biologists and volunteers united to collect, identify, and record various species that were found onsite including aquatic macroinvertebrates, fungi, mammals, and more. There were also activities and workshops for people of all ages to inform attendees of all that can be found in the area.

SWDR had a table at the ribbon cutting for the Lower Mount Bethel Township Tekening Trail System and Nature Center on June 20. This event was held in conjunction with day 9 of the Delaware River Sojourn. We greeted the 100+ sojourners as they came off the river. Click here http://www.bluevalleytimes.com/paper.html to view local media coverage of the event.
